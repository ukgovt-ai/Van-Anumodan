1. What is Van Anumodan?

It is a helper for checking forest land diversion proposals before the State Government forwards them. It walks you through a complete list of things that should be verified, keeps your findings in one place, points out gaps and mismatches, and produces a tidy scrutiny report.

Is it part of PARIVESH?

No. It is a separate, in-house quality-check tool. It is not connected to PARIVESH and does not submit, forward, or approve anything. You still do all official work on PARIVESH; this app just helps you scrutinise carefully first.

Will it decide the case for me?

No. It gives an advisory opinion and highlights problems. The decision always remains with the officer and competent authority.

2. What it can and cannot do

✓ What it can do

  • Give you the full scrutiny checklist (over 160 checkpoints) in one place.
  • Show only the checks that apply to your particular proposal.
  • Let you mark each check, add remarks, and record evidence.
  • Compare the area figures you enter and flag any mismatch.
  • Keep a running list of “red flags” automatically.
  • Tell you whether the proposal looks ready, and exactly what is blocking it.
  • Produce a scrutiny report and a deficiency memo you can print or save as PDF.
  • Hold a library of the relevant Acts, Rules and guidelines for reference.
  • Work offline, on your iPad, once installed.

✕ What it cannot do

  • It does not grant forest clearance or make the legal decision.
  • It does not read the proposal’s documents for you — you examine the proposal and enter the facts; the app checks them.
  • It does not draw maps or run GIS analysis; you record the GIS results you obtained elsewhere.
  • It does not connect to PARIVESH or submit anything.
  • It does not give legal advice — the rules must be verified and approved by the competent authority.
  • It does not search the internet on its own for rule changes (an administrator publishes updates).
  • It does not store your work in the cloud — data stays on the device you use.

3. Getting started & installing

How do I open it?

Open the web address given to you in a browser (on iPad, use Safari).

How do I install it like an app on my iPad?

  1. Open the address in Safari.
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow).
  3. Choose Add to Home Screen, then tap Add.
  4. A “Van Anumodan” icon appears on your home screen. Open it like any app — it runs full-screen.

On an Android phone or a computer with Chrome, an Install app button appears at the top — tap it.

4. Your data & internet

Do I need internet to use it?

Only the first time you open it. After that it works offline. Internet is useful for getting the latest rules and for opening source links.

Where is my scrutiny work saved?

On the device you are using, automatically, as you go. It is not sent anywhere. If you use another device, your work will not appear there unless you move it (see Reports & sharing a case).

If I close the app, do I lose my work?

No. It is saved on the device. To keep a backup or move it, use Export case.

Tip. When you are just trying the app inside a browser preview window, saving may not stick between reloads. Once it is properly installed / opened from its own web address, saving works normally.

5. Doing a scrutiny, step by step

  1. Proposal tab — type in the proposal details (PARIVESH number, name, areas, division, etc.).
  2. Tick the applicability flags that fit this case (for example, “Compensatory afforestation applies”, “Trees affected”, “Violation exists”) and pick the project category. This makes the app show only the relevant checks.
  3. Checklist tab — go through each checkpoint. For each one, tap Compliant, Non-Compliant, Clarification or Not Applicable, and add a remark if needed.
  4. Area Reconciliation tab — enter the area figure from each source. The app compares them and marks any mismatch.
  5. CA Registry / GIS / Doc Reconciliation tabs — fill these where they apply.
  6. Dashboard — see the overall verdict and what still needs attention.
  7. Reports tab — generate the scrutiny report or deficiency memo when done.

You don’t have to finish in one sitting — your progress is saved as you go.

What is the “applicability flag” for?

Different proposals need different checks. The flags switch on only the checks that matter for your case, so you are not bothered by irrelevant ones. If you are unsure, leave a check applicable and mark it — the app will not quietly assume something passed.

6. Statuses, colours & severity

Each check can be set to:

Compliant — done / satisfactory Clarification — needs more information Non-Compliant — a problem Not Applicable — doesn’t apply

What do “Critical” and “Major” mean?

Critical checks are serious — if any applicable Critical check is unresolved, the proposal cannot be shown as ready. Major checks matter too, but will show as “needs review” rather than a hard block.

7. Overrides & speaking orders

Sometimes an officer decides a flagged point is acceptable for a reasoned cause. In that case you can turn on Override for that check and write the reason (a speaking order). This lets the proposal move forward while keeping a clear, recorded justification.

Can everything be overridden?

No. The statutory “stop conditions” and final State-level gates cannot be overridden — these are hard legal bars, and the app will not allow it.

8. The verdict explained

The Dashboard shows one of three results, with the reasons listed:

Ready for Forwarding Requires Officer Review Not Ready for Forwarding

Ready = every applicable Critical check is settled and nothing is blocking. Requires Officer Review = the serious items are fine, but some checks are still open or unchecked. Not Ready = there is at least one unresolved Critical problem or stop condition, which is listed for you.

There is a percentage score — is that the decision?

No. The percentage is just a rough progress indicator for information. It does not decide readiness; the checks and stop conditions do.

9. Reports & sharing a case

How do I get a report?

On the Reports tab, tap Executive scrutiny report or Deficiency memo, then Print / save PDF to keep or share it.

How do I move my work to another device or back it up?

Tap Export case (JSON) to save a file of everything. On the other device, open the app and tap Import case and choose that file.

How do I start a fresh proposal?

Export the current case first if you want to keep it, then tap Reset case.

10. The Rules Library

The Rules Library tab lists the Acts, Rules and guidelines behind the checks, with links to the official documents. It shows a version and date at the top.

Who keeps the rules up to date?

A designated administrator updates them and publishes the update; every installed device then picks it up automatically the next time it is online. The app never changes the rules on its own from the internet.

There is an “Assisted rule intake” — what is that? (for administrators)

It helps turn an Act/Rule/amendment into a library entry. You can upload a text-based PDF or paste the text, and the app fills in the name, number, dates and clause for you to review and correct before saving. It only pre-fills — nothing is added without your approval. Scanned (photo) PDFs have no readable text, so paste the text instead.

11. Common problems

The logo screen appears every time I open it.

That is the normal opening screen; it disappears in about a second, or tap it to skip.

My changes didn’t save.

Make sure you opened the installed app (or its own web address), not a temporary preview window. Installed, it saves automatically.

A PDF wouldn’t read in the rule intake.

It is probably a scanned image with no text. Paste the text instead, or ask the administrator.

It asked me to reload for a new version.

That is a normal update — tap yes. Your saved work is not affected.

A rule’s web link won’t open in the app.

Some government sites block being read inside another app. Open the link in a normal browser tab.

12. Important cautions

This is a decision-support aid, not the decision. Always apply your own judgement and the applicable law.

Rules shown here must be verified against the official source and approved by the competent authority before they are relied upon.

Proposal information can be sensitive — treat the device and any exported files with the same care as official records.

The app helps you be thorough and consistent; it does not replace the officer, the Nodal establishment, or statutory procedure.
